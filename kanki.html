KAnki: Universal Flashcard App for Kindle

KAnki is a powerful, open-source flashcard application that brings true spaced repetition learning to your Kindle. Designed for jailbroken devices, KAnki helps you master vocabulary in any language—right from your e-reader.
What Is KAnki?
KAnki is inspired by the popular Anki flashcard software but made to run natively on Kindle e-readers. It supports fully customizable vocabulary decks, a spaced repetition system to make learning stick, and tracks your progress automatically. With universal language support, you can use KAnki to learn almost any language, whether you’re studying Japanese, Spanish, Mandarin, or anything else.
Features
  • Universal language support: Add your own language font and vocabulary
  • Spaced repetition system: Study smarter with proven memory techniques
  • Customizable flashcards: Organize cards by level (JLPT, CEFR, HSK, etc.)
  • Filtering by level: Focus on the cards you need
  • Star/favorite system: Mark and quickly find your most important cards
  • Reversible cards: Practice in both directions (native↔target)
  • Progress tracking: Track your study stats and review history
  • Error review mode: Instantly review cards you missed
  • Centralized configuration: Easy single-file setup
  • Optimized for e-ink: Minimal screen refreshes for Kindle models
  • Automatic data saving: All progress is saved locally—no internet or account needed
Requirements
  • A jailbroken Kindle e-reader
  • Access to your Kindle’s file system (USB connection to a computer)
How to Install KAnki (First Time Setup)
  1. Download the latest KAnki release from GitHub.
  2. Connect your Kindle to your computer via USB.
  3. Unzip the downloaded file. Make sure the folder is named KAnki.
  4. Copy the KAnki folder and kanki.sh script to the documents folder on your Kindle.
  5. Customize for your language:
    • Open kanki/js/kanki_config.js and edit the configuration for your language and vocabulary.
    • Download or convert a TTF font that supports your target language.
    • Rename the font to language.ttf and place it in kanki/assets/fonts/language.ttf.
  6. Disconnect your Kindle from the computer.
  7. Find and launch the KAnki app from your Kindle’s home screen.
How to Use KAnki
  • Study your cards: Use the touchscreen to reveal answers, star important words, and track your progress.
  • Switch direction: Practice both directions (e.g., Spanish→English and English→Spanish) with the reversible card feature.
  • Filter by level: Study words grouped by proficiency or exam level.
  • Star your favorites: Mark key words for quick review.
  • Review your stats: Check how many times you’ve seen each card and your review history.
  • Reset if needed: Use app options to clear your progress or return to a fresh deck.
How to Update KAnki
  1. Back up your current vocabulary or config files.
  2. Replace your old KAnki folder with the new version.
  3. Copy over your data and font.
  4. Launch the app and hit the Reload button in the menu to apply changes.
Customizing for Your Language
  1. Add a compatible font: Place your language’s TTF font in kanki/assets/fonts/language.ttf
  2. Edit the configuration:
    • Open kanki/js/kanki_config.js
    • Change the language name, proficiency levels, and vocabulary data
    • For languages with special scripts, add a reading field as needed
Example entry for a deck: 
var KANKI_CONFIG = {
  language: "Spanish",
  levels: ["A1", "A2", "B1"]
};
var VOCABULARY = {
  "A1": [
    {"front": "hello", "back": "hola", "notes": "Greeting"}
  ],
  "A2": [
    {"front": "tomorrow", "back": "mañana", "notes": "Time"}
  ]
};
Data and Progress
All your study progress, stats, and favorites are saved locally on your Kindle. You can reset your data at any time through the app or by deleting the storage file.
Technical Details & Limitations
  • KAnki is optimized for the limited browser and storage system on Kindle
  • Uses older JavaScript (ES5) and basic CSS for maximum compatibility
  • Not all modern web features are supported
  • Some Kindle screen sizes may cause minor layout issues, but the app works on most models
About the Project
KAnki is open-source and inspired by the Anki learning system. It’s built for the Kindle jailbreak community by independent developers and is under active development, with new features and fixes added regularly.

With KAnki, your Kindle becomes a portable, distraction-free flashcard trainer—perfect for language learners, test prep, or anyone who wants to remember more, right from their e-reader.
